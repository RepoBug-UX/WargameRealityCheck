Structured comparisons

The matcher found semantically related markets but the comparison is not apples-to-apples. The most common case is a wargame branch that is conditional on a precipitating event (e.g., "if invasion, will Japan grant basing?") matched against an unconditional market (e.g., "will the invasion occur?"). These pairs do not produce a single disagreement number; the structured comparison preserves both numbers and labels the difference.
